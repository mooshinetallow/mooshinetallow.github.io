Work

During the three summers in between my college semesters, I completed three co-op rotations with Midmark, a medical device company, as a Firmware Engineer. In these co-ops, I wrote code to catch errors for autoclaves, designed and assembled test fixtures for medical beds containing multiple PCBs, motors, a wireless handheld controller, and power and data transfer wiring. For my later rotations, I focused on UI and worked on the first-ever touchscreen that was being utilized at the company. I pioneered development in Qt and QML to design and develop workflows for a next generation autoclave.

Following graduation, I started a full-time position at Medpace, a contract research organization, as a UI/Software Engineer. As the first hire specializing in UI, I lead discussion and direction on our modernization project. I started out working on a design system for the department as a whole, before switching to leading a development project.

Concurrently, I honed my design and managerial skills in my free time while cofounding a social networking app, Linxy, with my brother. Here I led product development, designing sleek, succinct wireframes while exploring new features and direction for our product.

Education

Before university, I developed my skills through personal projects like modding, hardware, and personal app development. While at the University of Dayton, I focused my studies on Human Centered Design and User Interfaces pursuing a degree in Computer Engineering. While studying, I also conducted research for an Air Force contractor and developed a user-friendly GUI for polarimetric imaging software. Explore my portfolio to see how my technical expertise and design skills converge to create impactful and intuitive solutions in my work.
